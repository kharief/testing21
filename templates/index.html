    About Us



    Located in a picturesque environment in the heart of Kingston, Jamaica, the University of the West Indies (UWI) is one of the renowned, premier, and sought-after public universities. The University is dedicated to making a difference in higher education, with its exclusive approach towards the ongoing momentum of trends in technology and holistic development.

    Beginning as the University College of the West Indies in 1948, UWI has today grown into a full-fledged regional institution. The University proudly bears the legacy of its founders and continues to embody their impassioned faith in highly-qualified and fully-trained professionals in the service of the Caribbean region.

    The University started with only one programme in Medicine with an intake capacity of 33 students. Over the years, UWI has expanded its academic offerings significantly. In 1961, the Faculty of Social Sciences was established with programmes in Economics, Sociology, and Government. In 1966, the Faculty of Law was introduced, followed by the Faculty of Engineering in 1975. The 1980s saw the introduction of the Faculty of Education and the Faculty of Pure and Applied Sciences. The University has continually added new programmes to meet the demands of an evolving society.

    In recent years, UWI has started offering Master's and Doctoral programmes in various disciplines, including Business, Education, and the Sciences. The University has also introduced new undergraduate programmes in emerging areas such as Information Technology, Environmental Studies, and Biotechnology.

    Based on rigorous academic standards, several programmes of the University have been accredited by national and international accrediting bodies. UWI's commitment to excellence is reflected in its consistent ranking among the top universities in the Caribbean and Latin America. The University is ranked among the top 1000 universities globally by the QS World University Rankings. Additionally, the University is accredited by the Accreditation Council of Trinidad and Tobago (ACTT) and recognised for its quality education by the Caribbean Accreditation Authority for Education in Medicine and other Health Professions (CAAM-HP).

    UWI has been consistently ranked highly in major surveys and is recognised for its contributions to research and innovation. The University is renowned for its vibrant campus life, diverse student body, and commitment to regional development.
